## The fiscal problem (in one line)

NYC pays recurring housing subsidy costs without building durable household stability or any municipal "return"; PETF redirects a portion of that spend into a controlled, measurable stabilization instrument.

---

## What PETF does (mechanism, not ideology)

PETF converts a subset of eligible subsidy streams into **shared-equity, permanently affordable tenant ownership pathways**, implemented with:

* **Community Land Trust (CLT) ground-lease structure** to cap speculation and preserve affordability.
* **Mission lending (credit unions/CDFIs)** to standardize underwriting for low-income borrowers.
* **Program office + annual reporting** to enforce eligibility, equity targets, default mitigation, and outcomes.

---

## Why OMB should care

**1) Cost control:** Converts open-ended, recurring rent spend into a structured investment with performance gates.  
**2) Risk reduction:** Stabilization reduces expensive downstream costs (shelter entry, emergency placements, repeated court cycles).  
**3) Auditable governance:** Annual disaggregated reporting + independent evaluation creates budget-grade accountability.

---

## Guardrails (the "OMB comfort" section)

**Pilot gating (no blank checks):**

* Start with limited cohort size and predetermined buildings/targets.
* Require quarterly fiscal reporting and default/arrears monitoring.
* Triggered pause/stop rules if thresholds are missed (conversion rate, default, compliance).

**Legal + compliance integrity:**

* Uniform affordability covenant language.
* Fair housing review and disparate-impact monitoring.
* Procurement + conflicts controls for partner selection.

**Default mitigation built in:**

* Forbearance and modification protocols.
* Right-to-counsel linkage for program participants.
* Resale/transfer restrictions to prevent extraction.
