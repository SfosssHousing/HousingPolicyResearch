## SITUATION

NYC spends **$3.9B annually** on rental subsidies (Section 8, public housing, supportive housing) serving 400,000+ households. **Tenants build zero equity.** NYCHA faces $78B capital deficit. Rent-to-income ratios hit 55% citywide—double the affordability threshold.

**Problem:** Current subsidy model is a perpetual expense with no asset return or intergenerational wealth transfer.

______________________________________________________________________

## SOLUTION (OVERVIEW)

**Redeployment Model:** Reallocate 25% of Housing Assistance Payments (HAP) from traditional rent subsidy into **Tenant Equity Vehicles (TEVs)**—monthly equity accounts that accumulate toward ownership.

- Tenant still pays 30% of income (unchanged)
- City contributes $250–$400/month to equity account (from existing HAP)
- After 15 years: Tenant owns property (with CLT/deed restrictions for permanent affordability)
- **City cost:** Same ($3.9B/year); **Returns:** $850M–$1.2B in tenant wealth + property appreciation (15-year NPV)

## LEGAL STATUS

✅ **Feasible** under:

- Section 8(y) Housing Choice Voucher Homeownership Option (federal precedent)
- NYCHA RAD/PACT authority (existing)
- Section 504, Title VI, Fair Housing Act (with standard compliance)

⚠️ **Requires** clarification:

- NY Housing Stability & Tenant Protection Act (HSTPA) amendment to distinguish equity allocation from rent
- HPD term-sheet revision + NYCHA master lease amendment
- Municipal ordinance authorizing Tenant Equity Fund (TEF)
