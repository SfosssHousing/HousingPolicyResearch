TCAP

Tenant Co-Op Acquisition Program

Transforming NYC Rental Subsidies into Permanent Homeownership

NYC Housing Subsidy Policy Reform | December 2025

Submitted to: NYC Mayor's Office | Policy Analyst

The Problem

$37.5B annually spent on rental subsidies—but tenants never build equity.

  • No permanent solution: Vouchers subsidize rent indefinitely, creating dependency
  • Wealth gap: Homeownership rate for Black/Latino families: 19-20% vs. White families: 74%
  • Budget inefficiency: Subsidy costs increase 3-4% annually; buildings remain unaffordable
  • Eviction crisis: 300,000+ eviction filings annually despite subsidies

Current system: Perpetual renting. No ownership. No wealth.

The TCAP Solution

Convert rental subsidies into equity investments through tenant-owned cooperatives.

Phase 1: Acquisition

HPD acquires 353 buildings (10-year program) from current subsidy budget.

Phase 2: Co-Op Conversion

Tenants organize as cooperative, elect board, control building operations.

Phase 3: Financing

Credit union provides 30-year mortgages secured by real estate equity.

Phase 4: Permanent Homeownership

Co-op members own building forever, pass to heirs, build intergenerational wealth.
