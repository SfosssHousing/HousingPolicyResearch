# LOCAL LAW D: TENANT CO-OP ACQUISITION PROGRAM (TCAP) IMPLEMENTATION & OVERSIGHT

## Revised with "Member (Tenant-Owner)" Terminology

**Status:** Ready for NYC Council Introduction\
**Effective Date:** 180 days after enactment (aligned with LOCAL LAW A)

______________________________________________________________________

## SECTION 1: PROGRAM ESTABLISHED (§ 26-2001)

There is hereby established within the Department of Housing Preservation and Development (HPD) the **Tenant Co-op Acquisition Program (TCAP)**, which shall:

(a) Implement the Public Equity Transfer Framework (LOCAL LAW A) by facilitating conversion of rental housing subsidies into member (tenant-owner) equity and democratic cooperative control;

(b) Coordinate all tenant cooperative acquisition initiatives across HPD, DSS, NYCHA, and DHCR;

(c) Administer the Tenant Opportunity Fund to provide acquisition financing, subsidies, and technical assistance;

(d) Promote permanent affordability and prevent displacement by enabling tenant-led, member (tenant-owner)-controlled housing;

(e) Track program performance, member (tenant-owner) outcomes, and fiscal impact through data systems and annual reporting;

(f) Report to the Mayor and City Council on program implementation, barriers, and recommendations for scaling.

## SECTION 2: TCAP TASK FORCE (§ 26-2002)

### (a) Creation and Purpose

There is hereby established a **TCAP Task Force** to oversee program implementation, coordinate interagency collaboration, and provide strategic guidance on member (tenant-owner) acquisition and cooperative development.

### (b) Composition

The Task Force shall consist of:

1. **HPD Commissioner** (or designee), serving as Chair;

1. **DSS Commissioner** (or designee) — subsidy administration and member income verification;

1. **New York State DHCR Representative** — cooperative law and state program coordination;

1. **Mayor's Office of Housing Recovery Operations Representative** — housing strategy alignment;

1. **Three Tenant Representatives** appointed by City Council Speaker — community voice and member advocacy;

1. **Two CDFI/Credit Union Representatives** — acquisition financing expertise;

1. **Two Legal Service Provider Representatives** — member (tenant-owner) representation and dispute resolution;

1. **Two Cooperative Housing Advocacy Organization Representatives** — governance and member education;

1. **One Labor Union Representative** with housing expertise — workforce and community development.

### (c) Task Force Duties

The Task Force shall:

1. Develop implementation protocols, operational procedures, and member (tenant-owner) governance standards;

1. Establish pilot site selection criteria aligned with displacement risk, subsidy concentration, and organizing capacity;

1. Review and approve certification of technical assistance providers;

1. Monitor program performance and member (tenant-owner) outcomes through quarterly data review;

1. Recommend policy adjustments, regulatory changes, and scaling strategies;

1. Coordinate interagency collaboration and resolve jurisdictional conflicts;

1. Issue annual public reports on TCAP performance and recommendations;

1. Hold regular meetings (at least quarterly) and public hearings (at least semi-annually).

### (d) Member (Tenant-Owner) Participation

The Task Force shall:

1. Include member (tenant-owner) representatives from pilot cooperatives once they are operational;

1. Conduct quarterly listening sessions with tenant associations and early-stage member (tenant-owner) groups;

1. Solicit feedback on barriers, successes, and member satisfaction;

1. Make meeting agendas and minutes publicly available 10 days in advance.

## SECTION 3: PILOT PROGRAM PHASE (§ 26-2003)

### (a) Initial Implementation — Five-Year Pilot

TCAP shall begin with a structured **five-year pilot phase** involving a minimum of **five eligible properties** distributed across multiple boroughs, selected by the Task Force using criteria below.

### (b) Pilot Site Selection Criteria

Pilot sites shall be selected based on:

1. **High Subsidy Concentration:** Minimum 40% of households receiving CityFHEPS, Section 8, HASA, or equivalent subsidies;

1. **Documented Landlord Neglect:** History of housing code violations, hazardous conditions, or pattern of non-compliance;

1. **Tenant Organizing Capacity:** Existing tenant association, documented resident organizing, or demonstrated member interest;

1. **Geographic Diversity:** Selection across Bronx, Brooklyn, Manhattan, Queens to test multiple contexts;

1. **Financial Feasibility:** Property valuation, mortgage status, and conversion feasibility analysis indicate cooperative acquisition is financially viable;

1. **Community Context:** Property located in community experiencing high displacement risk or with active member (tenant-owner) housing advocacy;

1. **Owner Willingness or Necessity:** Owner willing to negotiate or property eligible for acquisition via TOPA (LOCAL LAW C), COPA (Intro 902-A), or foreclosure.

### (c) Pilot Evaluation Framework

HPD shall conduct rigorous, independent evaluation of each pilot site including:

1. **Housing Stability Metrics:** Eviction rates, tenant turnover, length of tenure before/after conversion;

1. **Member (Tenant-Owner) Financial Outcomes:** Equity account growth, carrying charge affordability, household income change;

1. **Cooperative Financial Performance:** Operating costs, reserve adequacy, debt service coverage, long-term sustainability;

1. **Member (Tenant-Owner) Governance Capacity:** Board election participation, member assembly attendance, voting rates, satisfaction surveys;

1. **Cost-Benefit Analysis:** Program cost per member (tenant-owner) vs. cost of traditional subsidy model, fiscal savings from reduced eviction/turnover;

1. **Lessons Learned and Best Practices:** Barriers to implementation, successful replication strategies, policy refinements;

1. **Comparative Analysis:** Performance vs. traditional rental subsidy cooperatives (if available) and DC TOPA cooperatives.

Evaluation shall be conducted by independent third-party evaluators and results published annually.

### (d) Pilot Timeline and Reporting

| Phase      | Duration     | Milestones                                                       |
| ---------- | ------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Year 1** | Months 1–12  | Site selection, member (tenant-owner) org, financing, conversion |
| **Year 2** | Months 13–24 | Member (tenant-owner) boarding, equity accumulation begins       |
| **Year 3** | Months 25–36 | Interim evaluation, governance training, policy refinement       |
| **Year 4** | Months 37–48 | Scaling planning, additional sites (if successful)               |
| **Year 5** | Months 49–60 | Final evaluation, citywide expansion recommendation              |

HPD shall submit interim evaluation reports to the Mayor and City Council by **June 30** of each year, with final report by **December 31 of Year 5**.

## SECTION 4: TENANT OPPORTUNITY FUND (§ 26-2004)

### (a) Fund Establishment

There is hereby established within HPD the **Tenant Opportunity Fund (TOF)** to support TCAP implementation through acquisition financing, member (tenant-owner) subsidy conversion, and technical assistance.

### (b) Funding Sources

The TOF shall consist of:

1. **City General Fund Appropriation:** Annual City appropriation of not less than $50 million (expanding to $150 million+ upon successful pilot evaluation);

1. **Federal Grants and Waivers:** HUD Moving to Work (MTW) waivers, Community Development Block Grant (CDBG), HOME funds, and other federal housing grants;

1. **New York State Appropriations:** NYS Division of Housing and Community Renewal allocations, state housing program reserves;

1. **Philanthropic Contributions:** Foundation grants, donor contributions directed to TCAP;

1. **Loan Repayments:** Principal and interest repayments from member (tenant-owner) cooperatives on acquisition and equity loans;

1. **Municipal Bonds:** General obligation or revenue bonds to support lending capacity;

1. **Other Sources:** Tax credit sales, forfeiited subsidies, agency reallocations, and other available resources.

### (c) Authorized Uses of Fund Resources

TOF funds may be used for:

1. **Acquisition Financing:** Below-market-rate acquisition loans to tenant associations and member (tenant-owner) cooperatives;

1. **Loan Guarantees and Credit Enhancements:** Guarantees to CDFI/credit union lenders to reduce interest rates and increase loan availability;

1. **Member (Tenant-Owner) Subsidy Conversion:** Direct grants or subsidies for member equity account seeding;

1. **Capital Improvement Grants:** Funding for urgent building repairs and capital improvements needed to support acquisition;

1. **Reserve Fund Seeding:** Establishment grants for cooperative operating and capital reserves;

1. **Technical Assistance and Legal Services:** Grants to cooperative development organizations, legal service providers for member (tenant-owner) support;

1. **Member (Tenant-Owner) Governance Training:** Cooperative leadership development, financial literacy, democratic procedures education;

1. **Program Administration:** HPD staff, data systems, evaluation, and management of TCAP;

1. **Emergency Stabilization:** Short-term financial support for member (tenant-owner) cooperatives experiencing hardship or financial distress;

1. **Member (Tenant-Owner) Hardship Support:** Individual member assistance for carrying-charge payment hardship or emergency relocation costs.

### (d) Loan Structure and Terms

Acquisition loans provided through TOF shall:

1. **Principal Limit:** Not exceed 95% of appraised property value;

1. **Interest Rate:** Carry fixed interest rates not to exceed 2% above the 10-year U.S. Treasury rate at time of closing;

1. **Term:** Maximum 30-year amortization;

1. **Grace Period:** Include minimum 2-year grace period for principal repayment during post-acquisition stabilization;

1. **Prepayment:** Allow prepayment without penalty;

1. **Collateral:** Be secured by first mortgage liens on the property and pledge of cooperative shares;

1. **Subordination:** HPD may agree to subordination of its lien to CDFI/credit union first mortgage lien;

1. **Flexibility:** Include flexible documentation for non-traditional borrowers and cooperative entities.

### (e) Revolving Loan Structure

The TOF shall operate as a **revolving loan fund** with:

1. Principal and interest repayments reinvested to originate new member (tenant-owner) cooperative acquisition loans;

1. Annual review of fund balance and capitalization, with appropriations adjusted to maintain lending capacity;

1. Public reporting on fund lending activity, delinquency rates, and cumulative member (tenant-owner) acquisition;

1. Authority to use any annual surplus for member (tenant-owner) assistance grants or technical assistance expansion.

## SECTION 5: INTERAGENCY AGREEMENTS (§ 26-2005)

### (a) Memoranda of Understanding

HPD shall execute comprehensive MOUs with:

1. **Department of Social Services** — Subsidy administration coordination, member (tenant-owner) income verification, data sharing protocols;

1. **New York State DHCR** — Cooperative law compliance, member (tenant-owner) governance standards, rent regulation;

1. **U.S. HUD** — Moving to Work (MTW) waivers, flexible subsidy deployment, federal reporting requirements;

1. **Community Development Financial Institutions** — Acquisition financing capacity, member (tenant-owner) loan products, interest rate standards;

1. **Legal Service Providers** — Member (tenant-owner) representation capacity, dispute resolution, governance training;

1. **Nonprofit Cooperative Development Organizations** — Technical assistance provider network, member (tenant-owner) education standards.

### (b) MOU Content Requirements

Each MOU shall specify:

1. Roles and responsibilities of each party;

1. Data sharing protocols and privacy safeguards;

1. Member (tenant-owner) service standards and performance metrics;

1. Dispute resolution procedures;

1. Funding commitments and resource allocation;

1. Annual review and amendment procedures;

1. Accountability mechanisms and enforcement.

## SECTION 6: DATA SYSTEMS AND TRANSPARENCY (§ 26-2006)

### (a) TCAP Data Management System

HPD shall establish an integrated **TCAP data management system** to track:

1. **Eligible Properties:** Address, number of units, subsidy concentration, ownership, contact information;

1. **Tenant Interest:** Tenant association formation, members (tenant-owners) expressing purchase intent, organizing status;

1. **Subsidy Conversion:** Monthly subsidy redirected to member (tenant-owner) equity accounts, allocation tracking (80/20 split verification);

1. **Member (Tenant-Owner) Equity:** Individual equity account balances, monthly accrual, cumulative member wealth accumulation;

1. **Cooperative Financial Performance:** Operating expenses, carrying charges, reserve balances, debt service, delinquency rates;

1. **Member (Tenant-Owner) Outcomes:** Eviction rates, occupancy tenure, household income, satisfaction surveys, retention;

1. **Program Costs:** Acquisition loan amounts, grant disbursements, technical assistance expenditure, administrative costs;

1. **Return on Investment (ROI):** Cost per member (tenant-owner), fiscal savings from reduced turnover/eviction vs. subsidy costs, long-term payback;

1. **Compliance and Enforcement:** Affordability covenant compliance, carrying-charge violations, member (tenant-owner) complaints, enforcement actions.

### (b) Public Website and Reporting

HPD shall publish on its website:

1. **Program Guidelines and Applications:** TCAP application materials, eligibility criteria, timelines;

1. **Certified Technical Assistance Providers:** Directory with contact information, specialization, capacity;

1. **Qualified Lenders:** Pre-qualified CDFI/credit union lenders, loan terms, application procedures;

1. **Quarterly Performance Metrics:** Number of sites, member (tenant-owners), equity accumulated, loan portfolio;

1. **Annual Evaluation Reports:** Pilot program outcomes, member (tenant-owner) satisfaction, barriers, lessons learned;

1. **Member (Tenant-Owner) Resources:** Plain-language guides, FAQs, governance training materials, financial literacy tools;

1. **Data Dashboard:** Interactive maps, charts, and downloadable datasets for researchers and advocates.

### (c) Annual Public Report

HPD shall publish **Annual TCAP Report** (by February 28) containing:

1. Number of eligible properties identified and outreach conducted;

1. Number of tenant associations certified and active;

1. Number of member (tenant-owner) cooperative acquisitions completed;

1. Total subsidy amounts redirected to member (tenant-owner) equity;

1. Member (tenant-owner) demographics (race, ethnicity, income, household size);

1. Member (tenant-owner) equity account growth (individual and aggregate);

1. Cooperative financial performance and sustainability metrics;

1. Member (tenant-owner) housing stability outcomes (eviction, turnover, satisfaction);

1. Fiscal impact on city and state budgets;

1. Comparison with traditional rental subsidy model;

1. Barriers to implementation and policy recommendations;

1. Scaling plan for post-pilot expansion.

## SECTION 7: COMMUNITY OUTREACH AND MEMBER (TENANT-OWNER) EDUCATION (§ 26-2007)

### (a) Outreach and Engagement

HPD shall conduct ongoing community outreach including:

1. **Multilingual Materials:** Informational materials, applications, member (tenant-owner) guides in English, Spanish, Mandarin, Korean, and other languages spoken by 10%+ of NYC renters;

1. **Community Workshops and Town Halls:** Quarterly events in each borough explaining TCAP, TOPA/COPA rights, cooperative basics, financing;

1. **Partnerships with Tenant Organizations:** Collaboration with housing advocacy groups, tenant unions, and community boards for outreach;

1. **Media Campaigns:** Video, social media, radio campaigns explaining member (tenant-owner) cooperative concept and TCAP eligibility;

1. **Targeted Outreach:** Door-to-door organizing in high-subsidy buildings, community centers, tenant meetings;

1. **Member (Tenant-Owner) Stories:** Case studies and testimonials from pilot site member (tenant-owners) highlighting benefits and governance.

### (b) Member (Tenant-Owner) Education and Training

HPD shall fund and certify technical assistance providers to deliver:

1. **Cooperative Governance Training:** Board roles, voting procedures, fiduciary duties, democratic decision-making;

1. **Financial Literacy:** Equity accounts, carrying charges, affordability covenants, long-term wealth accumulation;

1. **Affordable Housing Law:** Understanding TCAP, TOPA/COPA, affordability covenant enforcement, member rights;

1. **Building Operations:** Capital planning, reserve studies, maintenance, energy efficiency;

1. **Conflict Resolution:** Mediation skills, dispute resolution, addressing member (tenant-owner) grievances;

1. **Member (Tenant-Owner) Organizing:** Tenant association formation, membership recruitment, consensus-building.

All training shall be provided in multiple languages and accessible formats.

## SECTION 8: MEMBER (TENANT-OWNER) PROTECTIONS AND ANTI-DISCRIMINATION (§ 26-2008)

### (a) Anti-Discrimination and Fair Housing

TCAP shall comply with all federal, state, and local fair housing and anti-discrimination laws, including:

1. **Fair Housing Act** (42 U.S.C. § 3604);

1. **New York Human Rights Law** (NY Admin. Code § 8-102);

1. **ADA and Section 504** accessibility requirements;

1. **Language Access** provisions (Local Law 30).

No member (tenant-owner) shall be excluded or discriminated against based on race, color, national origin, gender, familial status, disability, age, sexual orientation, gender identity, or citizenship/immigration status.

### (b) Member (Tenant-Owner) Grievance and Dispute Resolution

HPD shall establish:

1. **Member (Tenant-Owner) Hotline:** Toll-free number for questions, complaints, and referrals;

1. **Complaint Investigation:** 30-day response time for member (tenant-owner) complaints regarding retaliation, affordability violations, governance abuse;

1. **Mediation Services:** Access to neutral mediators for member disputes with cooperatives;

1. **Legal Assistance:** Referrals to legal service providers for member (tenant-owner) representation;

1. **Enforcement:** Authority to impose penalties on cooperatives for member (tenant-owner) rights violations.

## SECTION 9: SUNSET, REVIEW, AND CONTINUATION (§ 26-2009)

### (a) Pilot Program Sunset

The five-year pilot program under § 26-2003 shall sunset on **December 31 of Year 5** unless:

1. The independent evaluation demonstrates positive member (tenant-owner) outcomes and financial sustainability;

1. The Task Force recommends continuation and scaling;

1. The City Council votes to extend or make the program permanent.

### (b) Mandatory Review and Recommendations

By **June 1 of Year 5**, HPD shall submit to the Mayor and City Council:

1. Final independent evaluation of all pilot sites;

1. Member (tenant-owner) outcome data and long-term sustainability analysis;

1. Comparative analysis with DC TOPA cooperatives and similar models;

1. Fiscal impact assessment and ROI analysis;

1. Barriers encountered and policy recommendations;

1. Scaling plan for citywide expansion (if positive recommendation);

1. Legislative recommendations for permanent authorization.

### (c) Continuation and Expansion Authority

If the evaluation is positive, the City Council may by resolution:

1. Authorize continuation of the TCAP program beyond the pilot phase;

1. Appropriate expanded funding for member (tenant-owner) cooperative acquisition citywide;

1. Amend this chapter to add permanent authorization language;

1. Direct HPD to scale the program to reach 500+ member (tenant-owner) cooperatives and 50,000+ member (tenant-owners) within 10 years.

## SECTION 10: RULEMAKING AND IMPLEMENTATION (§ 26-2010)

HPD Commissioner shall promulgate rules necessary to implement TCAP including:

(a) Application processes and member (tenant-owner) tenant association certification criteria;

(b) Financial underwriting standards for member (tenant-owner) qualification and cooperative feasibility;

(c) Affordability covenant templates and resale formula models;

(d) Member (tenant-owner) equity account reporting standards and quarterly statement requirements;

(e) Technical assistance provider certification and quality standards;

(f) Subsidy allocation procedures (80/20 split implementation);

(g) Member (tenant-owner) grievance and enforcement procedures;

(h) Data collection and reporting standards;

(i) Interagency coordination protocols;

(j) Language access and accessibility requirements for all member (tenant-owner) communications.
