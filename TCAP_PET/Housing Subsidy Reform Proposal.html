Housing Subsidy Reform

A Comprehensive Policy Proposal for NYC

Policy Analysis for Housing Justice & Affordability • December 2024

The Housing Affordability Crisis

  • Over 2 million NYC households spend >30% of income on rent (cost-burdened)
  • 336,000 households on HCV waitlist; average wait 8+ years
  • NYC receives $7.2B in federal housing assistance annually
  • Only 1 in 4 eligible low-income households receive any subsidy

Federal Housing Assistance Programs

Housing Choice Vouchers

$6.5B annually for 120,000+ NYC households

Public Housing

NYCHA manages 180,000+ units

LIHTC & Production

$833M annually for affordable housing development

Empirical Evidence: What Works

  • Voucher recipients 2-4x less likely to be housing-insecure vs. unassisted
  • Housing quality: 7.9% more voucher holders rate housing as "good/excellent"
  • Neighborhood outcomes: Voucher holders live in 5.2% lower-poverty areas
  • Children exposed to vouchers before age 13 have 30% higher earnings as adults

The Landlord Discrimination Problem

48% of NYC landlords exhibit source-of-income (SOI) discrimination

  • Nearly 50% of NYCHA voucher recipients lose vouchers unable to find housing
  • Sophisticated discrimination tactics: steering, ghosting, inflated credit requirements
  • Only 17 CCHR attorneys enforce fair housing for 8+ million New Yorkers
  • Underfunded CCHR leaves discrimination largely unpunished

Four Pillars of Reform

  • Pillar 1: Expand Voucher Access - Double HCV supply from 120,000 to 240,000+ units
  • Pillar 2: Eliminate Discrimination - Fund enforcement; implement landlord incentives
  • Pillar 3: Improve Payment Standards - Implement SAFMR in high-opportunity areas
  • Pillar 4: Support Implementation - Training, tech, data infrastructure

Pillar 1: Expanding Voucher Access

Target: Add 120,000 new vouchers over 5 years

  • Reopen waitlist for all eligible households (not lottery-closed)
  • Federal funding: $1.2B annually to HPD + NYCHA for expansion
  • Priority: 75% to extremely low-income (ELI) households per HUD mandate
  • Cost: ~$1,700/month per voucher ($20.4B over 10 years)

Pillar 2: Eliminating Discrimination

Two-pronged approach: Enforcement + Incentives

  • A. CCHR Funding: Increase from 17 to 50+ attorneys; $15M annually
  • B. Landlord Incentives: Tax credits, insurance protections for SOI participants
  • C. Proactive Testing: Fair Housing Initiative paired testing operations
  • D. Behavioral Interventions: Ideas42 model for landlord engagement

Pillar 3: Strategic Allocation Metrics (SAFMR)

Location-based rent standards to expand opportunity access

  • Implement SAFMR in 15 high-opportunity census tracts (low poverty, job centers, transit)
  • Evidence: 24 metro areas with SAFMR show voucher holders move to lower-poverty neighborhoods
  • Cost-neutral policy: Redirects existing HCV budget, not increased spending
  • Protects current holders: Grandfathered at existing payment standards

Pillar 4: Implementation Infrastructure

Systems and resources for program success

  • Tenant services: Dedicated counselors, language access, mobility coaching
  • Landlord engagement: Front-end relationship building and payment reliability assurance
  • Data systems: Real-time dashboards for waitlist management, utilization tracking
  • Staff capacity: HPD + NYCHA + CCHR hiring plan for administration

Budgetary Impact (10-Year Horizon)

  • Voucher Expansion (Pillar 1): $20.4B (federal + local match)
  • Enforcement (Pillar 2): $150M (CCHR + testing)
  • Administrative/Infrastructure (Pillar 4): $200M (systems, staff, training)
  • TOTAL 10-YEAR COST: ~$20.75B for permanent assistance to 240,000+ households
  • Per-household 10-year cost: ~$86,500 (highly cost-effective)

Legislative Implementation Strategy

Model: ROAD to Housing Act framework (2025)

  • State: "Housing Opportunity and Discrimination Prevention Act" (HOPA)
  • City: "Source of Income Enforcement and Voucher Expansion Law"
  • HUD: Emergency Rental Assistance modernization + HCV reauthorization
  • Timelines: Year 1-2 funding authorization; Years 3-5 full expansion

Equity Impact

  • 65% of HCV recipients nationwide are Black & Latinx; 78% are female-headed households
  • Reform prioritizes ELI households (75% of new vouchers)
  • Targets women, seniors, people with disabilities disproportionately excluded
  • Anti-discrimination enforcement protects civil rights of 120,000 new families

Implementation Risks & Mitigations

Risk: Federal Funding Cuts

Mitigation: State/city revenue bonds; phased expansion

Risk: Landlord Resistance

Mitigation: Incentives; enforcement; relationship building

Risk: Tight Rental Market

Mitigation: Concurrent production; concurrent affordability

Measurable Success Metrics (5-Year Targets)

  • 120,000+ new HCV vouchers issued to eligible households
  • 50,000+ new households moved to opportunity neighborhoods (SAFMR)
  • 50+ reduction in SOI discrimination complaints (enforcement effectiveness)
  • $100M+ in state/local tax credit investment to landlords accepting vouchers

Alignment with Federal Reform Momentum

ROAD to Housing Act 2025 & Administration Priorities

  • Build Now Act: Ties CDBG funding to housing production
  • Choice in Affordable Housing: Streamlines HCV inspection process
  • Innovation Fund: $200M for local demonstration of proven reforms
  • NYC proposal aligns with national evidence-based best practices

Call to Action

Implement comprehensive housing subsidy reform to:

  • House 120,000+ more families in stable, quality housing
  • Eliminate racial and source-of-income discrimination
  • Invest $20.75B over 10 years for permanent affordability
  • Lead the nation in tenant rights and housing justice
