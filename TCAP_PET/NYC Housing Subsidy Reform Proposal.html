NYC Housing Subsidy Reform

Public Equity Transfer Framework (PET)

Restructuring rental subsidies into tenant ownership, maximizing ROI and intergenerational equity while reusing existing agency budgets

NYC Department of Housing Preservation & Development | Submitted to Mayor-Elect Transition Team | December 2025

Executive Summary: The Problem

  • $5.7B annually spent on rental subsidies serving 455K households in temporary solutions
  • Zero cumulative equity: Rent payments disappear; tenants gain no ownership stake
  • Intergenerational displacement: Subsidies end; families exit housing market entirely
  • Efficiency gap: Administrative overhead (20-30%) vs. direct housing investment

Current Model Status:

NYC spends more on subsidies than Vienna, yet achieves lower affordability outcomes—because Vienna invests in public ownership.

The Solution: Public Equity Transfer (PET)

1. Redirect Subsidies

Convert HRA rental vouchers into down-payment reserves managed by member-owned credit unions.

2. Build Tenant Equity

Accrue 20-40% ownership equity in 10-15 years through TCAP (Tenant Credit Accumulation Program).

3. Generate ROI

Reuse $600M annually for new affordable housing, reducing net cost per household by 35%.

TCAP: Tenant Credit Accumulation Program

Year 1-5

  • Monthly rent payment = 30% AMI
  • Subsidy converts to equity reserve
  • Credit building (credit union membership)
  • 15% ownership stake accumulation

Year 6-10

  • Option to purchase 25-40% stake
  • Mortgage rates: 1-2% (below market)
  • $50K-$80K equity accumulated
  • Refinance option at year 10

Year 11+

  • Path to majority ownership (60%+)
  • Co-op governance participation
  • Intergenerational transfer eligible
  • $120K-$180K total equity

International Models: Vienna, Singapore, Toronto

Vienna (62% Social Housing)

Municipal cooperatives own 38% of stock; rents @ 25% AMI; intergenerational transfer model; $2.8B annual ops budget serving 250K HHs

Singapore (80% Public Housing)

HDB program: tenant purchase options (99-year lease); 90% homeownership rate; CPF (retirement) contributions subsidize down payments

Toronto (Inclusionary Zoning)

15% inclusion mandate in new developments; CICs (Community Investment Cooperatives) own 12% of affordable stock; average 18% equity accrual in 10 years

Proposed Local Law: Key Provisions

§ 1. Authority & Definitions

HPD authorized to establish TCAP and PET framework; defines "equity accrual," "member-owned credit union," "affordability lock" (permanent).

§ 2. Subsidy Redirection & MOA

HRA redirects monthly voucher + security deposit amounts to HPD-designated credit union accounts; credit union operates under HPD oversight MOU.

§ 3. Tenant Rights & Protections

Affirmative enrollment rights; anti-displacement protections; annual equity disclosure; ombudsman access; intergenerational transfer eligibility.

§ 4. Reinvestment Authority

Recovered subsidy savings may be reinvested in new affordable construction, subject to City Council approval and equity impact review.

Literature Review: Evidence Base

Community Land Trusts

Credit Union Models

CUNA & Filene (2024): Member-owned credit union underwriting matches or exceeds bank risk profiles; operational cost ~40% lower.

Racial Wealth Gap

Vienna Model

Hegedüs & Tosics (2018): Vienna's social housing achieves 62% stock coverage at lower cost than NYC subsidies; intergenerational stability high.
