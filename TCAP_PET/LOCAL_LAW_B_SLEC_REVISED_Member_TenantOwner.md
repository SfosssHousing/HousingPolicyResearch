# LOCAL LAW B: SUBSIDIZED LIMITED-EQUITY COOPERATIVE (SLEC) CLASSIFICATION

## Revised with "Member (Tenant-Owner)" Terminology

**Status:** Ready for NYC Council Introduction\
**Effective Date:** Same date as LOCAL LAW A

______________________________________________________________________

## SECTION 1: SHORT TITLE

This section may be cited as the **Subsidized Limited-Equity Cooperative (SLEC) Classification and Regulatory Framework**.

______________________________________________________________________

## SECTION 2: CLASSIFICATION ESTABLISHMENT (§ 26-301.1)

### (a) Housing Classification Established

There is hereby established a housing classification known as **Subsidized Limited-Equity Cooperatives (SLECs)**, comprised of housing cooperatives in which:

1. Member (tenant-owner) shareholders own and occupy residential units;

1. Shares are restricted to preserve permanent affordability through articles of incorporation, bylaws, or affordability covenant deed restrictions;

1. Member (tenant-owner) carrying charges do not exceed 15% of household income, indexed annually;

1. At least 40% of member (tenant-owner) households utilize convertible public housing equity instruments (subsidies) for equity accumulation; and

1. Affordability covenants run with the land for a minimum of 30 years.

______________________________________________________________________

## SECTION 3: MEMBER (TENANT-OWNER) CERTIFICATION AND REGISTRATION (§ 26-301.1(b))

### (a) Registration Requirement

All SLECs shall register with the Department of Housing Preservation and Development (HPD) within 90 days of completing property acquisition and shall maintain current registration annually.

### (b) Member (Tenant-Owner) Certification

Each member (tenant-owner) in an SLEC shall complete HPD certification demonstrating:

1. **Income Verification:** Annual household income at or below area median income (AMI) thresholds designated by HPD;

1. **Equity Account Documentation:** Current balance and monthly accrual records for individual member equity accounts;

1. **Occupancy Status:** Primary occupancy in the designated unit;

1. **Affordability Compliance:** Carrying charge does not exceed 15% of verified household income;

1. **Governance Participation:** Member participation in cooperative governance meetings, voting, and training.

### (c) Recertification

Member (tenant-owner) recertifications shall occur annually, aligned with subsidy recertification cycles through the Department of Social Services (DSS).

## SECTION 4: REGULATORY OVERSIGHT (§ 26-301.1(c))

### (a) Annual Financial Audits

All SLECs shall undergo annual financial audits by independent certified public accountants, including:

1. Member (tenant-owner) equity account verification and reconciliation;

1. Carrying-charge compliance review (15% income cap);

1. Affordability covenant covenant compliance documentation;

1. Reserve account balances and allocation review;

1. Mortgage and debt service payment verification.

### (b) Governance Compliance Reviews

HPD shall conduct periodic reviews to ensure:

1. Member (tenant-owner) voting rights are exercised democratically (one vote per share);

1. Board elections occur annually with member (tenant-owner) participation;

1. Annual member assemblies are held with adequate notice and participation;

1. Bylaws provide for democratic amendment procedures;

1. Conflict-of-interest and ethical standards are maintained.

### (c) Affordability Covenant Enforcement

HPD shall monitor and enforce affordability covenants, including:

1. Enforcement of 30-year minimum affordability period;

1. Resale price formula compliance (limits on member appreciation);

1. Occupancy requirements (member occupancy vs. rental);

1. Prevention of conversion to market-rate units.

### (d) Periodic Site Inspections

HPD shall conduct annual physical inspections of SLEC buildings to verify:

1. Habitability standards compliance;

1. Building code violations and remediation status;

1. Capital improvement readiness and reserve adequacy;

1. Safety and common area maintenance.

### (e) Member (Tenant-Owner) Complaint Investigations

HPD shall establish a complaint process for members (tenant-owners) alleging:

1. Retaliation for exercising member rights;

1. Violation of affordability covenants;

1. Improper carrying-charge assessments;

1. Governance irregularities or board misconduct;

1. Breach of member protections under this chapter.

Complaints shall be investigated within 30 days and resolved within 60 days, with HPD authority to impose penalties and seek injunctive relief.

## SECTION 5: TAX TREATMENT FOR COMPLIANT SLECs (§ 26-301.1(d))

### (a) Real Property Tax Exemption Eligibility

SLECs that maintain compliance with affordability covenants and member (tenant-owner) protections shall be eligible for:

1. **Full or Partial Real Property Tax Exemption** pursuant to Article XI of the New York Private Housing Finance Law or successor provisions;

1. **Cooperative Housing Tax Credit** under New York State Tax Law § 606-a;

1. **Property Tax Relief Programs** for affordable housing cooperatives.

### (b) Continued Eligibility for Existing Tax Incentives

SLECs shall retain eligibility for:

1. Affordable housing tax incentive programs;

1. Low-income housing tax credit (LIHTC) partnerships or designations;

1. 421-a or equivalent affordable housing tax incentive programs (if applicable upon acquisition);

1. Community Development Financial Institution (CDFI) lending rate reductions.

### (c) Additional Tax Benefits

SLECs that meet or exceed affordability and member (tenant-owner) governance standards shall be eligible for:

1. Tax exemptions on property transfers during member (tenant-owner) turnover;

1. Reduced or waived filing fees for deed restrictions and regulatory compliance documents;

1. Such other tax benefits as may be established by city, state, or federal law.

## SECTION 6: EXEMPTION FROM CONVERSION REQUIREMENTS (§ 26-301.1(e))

### (a) Exemption from General Business Law Article 5

SLECs established pursuant to LOCAL LAW A (PETF) shall be exempt from the conversion requirements of New York General Business Law Article 5 (cooperative conversion notice and approval procedures), provided that:

1. **Tenant Initiative:** The conversion is initiated by tenant associations or resident groups, not imposed by the property owner;

1. **Full Disclosure:** All tenants receive full written disclosure of conversion terms, financing, carrying charges, and member rights and obligations;

1. **Participation Opportunity:** All tenant households are given equal opportunity to participate in formation decisions and to purchase shares;

1. **Non-Purchasing Tenant Protection:** Tenants who choose not to purchase shares retain occupancy rights and continued subsidy support with non-eviction protection during a transition period of not less than two years;

1. **HPD Certification:** HPD certifies that the conversion complies with this chapter, LOCAL LAW A (PETF), LOCAL LAW C (TOPA), and LOCAL LAW D (TCAP Implementation).

### (b) Notice and Timeline Requirements

Tenant associations undertaking SLEC conversion under LOCAL LAW A shall:

1. Provide 120 days' notice to all tenant households of conversion intent;

1. Distribute disclosure documents in English and other languages spoken by 10%+ of tenants;

1. Hold at least three public member meetings to address conversion questions and concerns;

1. Document tenant participation and support (minimum 50% of households for conversion to proceed);

1. Submit HPD certification application before final share purchases.

## SECTION 7: MEMBER (TENANT-OWNER) PROTECTIONS WITHIN SLECs (§ 26-301.1(f))

### (a) Anti-Displacement and Non-Retaliation

No SLEC board, officer, or agent shall:

1. Evict or threaten to evict a member (tenant-owner) for exercising member rights;

1. Assess special charges or increase carrying charges in retaliation for member governance participation or complaints;

1. Restrict member access to financial records, meeting minutes, or decision-making processes;

1. Interfere with member (tenant-owner) organization or advocacy;

1. Discriminate against members based on race, color, national origin, gender, familial status, disability, age, sexual orientation, or immigration status.

### (b) Enforcement of Member (Tenant-Owner) Rights

Members (tenant-owners) have the right to:

1. **Occupancy:** Exclusive occupancy of their unit for the duration of membership;

1. **Voting:** One vote per share in cooperative governance;

1. **Information:** Access to cooperative financial records, board minutes, and decision-making materials;

1. **Equity:** Non-forfeitable equity account with quarterly statements and annual reporting;

1. **Grievance:** File complaints with HPD regarding covenant violations, retaliation, or governance abuse.

### (c) Penalties for Non-Compliance

SLECs in material violation of member (tenant-owner) protections shall be subject to:

1. Corrective action orders from HPD;

1. Civil penalties up to $10,000 per violation per day;

1. Suspension or loss of tax exemption status;

1. Removal of non-compliant board officers (with HPD authority to appoint interim board);

1. Receivership or judicial dissolution in cases of egregious or repeated violation.

## SECTION 8: INTERAGENCY COORDINATION (§ 26-301.1(g))

### (a) Coordination with HPD and DSS

The Department of Housing Preservation and Development shall coordinate with the Department of Social Services to:

1. Align SLEC member (tenant-owner) income verification with subsidy recertification;

1. Ensure subsidy payments flow to cooperatives per PETF allocation (80% operating / 20% member equity);

1. Provide member (tenant-owner) list to DSS for verification purposes;

1. Maintain confidentiality of member income information per applicable privacy laws.

### (b) Coordination with DHCR and State Housing Authorities

HPD shall coordinate with New York State Division of Housing and Community Renewal (DHCR) to:

1. Register SLECs under state cooperative housing program requirements;

1. Ensure compliance with NY Cooperative Law § 1801–2000;

1. Support SLEC member (tenant-owner) governance training;

1. Facilitate state tax credit and affordable housing program eligibility.

### (c) Coordination with Legal Service Providers

HPD shall maintain contracts with legal service providers to:

1. Represent members (tenant-owners) in disputes with cooperatives;

1. Review SLEC bylaws and affordability covenants for compliance;

1. Advise on member (tenant-owner) rights and governance procedures;

1. Provide dispute resolution and mediation services.

## SECTION 9: DATA COLLECTION AND TRANSPARENCY (§ 26-301.1(h))

### (a) SLEC Registry

HPD shall maintain a public registry of SLECs containing:

1. Property address and building identification;

1. Number of units and member (tenant-owner) households;

1. Affordability covenant period and terms;

1. Member (tenant-owner) income limits and carrying-charge cap;

1. Board contact information and annual certification status;

1. Links to cooperative bylaws and affordability covenant documents.

### (b) Annual Reporting

HPD shall publish annual reports on SLEC program performance including:

1. Number of SLECs operating citywide;

1. Number of members (tenant-owners) and household demographics;

1. Average member equity account balances and annual growth;

1. Member satisfaction and grievance data;

1. Housing stability metrics (eviction rates, occupancy turnover);

1. Tax benefits distributed to SLECs;

1. Compliance violations and enforcement actions;

1. Recommendations for policy improvements.

## SECTION 10: EFFECTIVE DATE

This local law takes effect on the same date as LOCAL LAW A (Public Equity Transfer Framework) becomes effective, 180 days after enactment. HPD shall take all actions necessary for implementation, including promulgation of rules and SLEC classification standards, prior to effective date.
