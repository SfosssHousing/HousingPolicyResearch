Housing Subsidy Reform (PET + TCAP)

This portfolio presents an implementation-first housing subsidy reform proposal grounded in administrative feasibility, program integrity, and public finance discipline. The core mechanism—PET (Public Equity Transfer)— is paired with an acquisition pathway, TCAP, to reorient recurring subsidy expenditures toward durable housing stability through controlled conversion, auditability, and measurable performance outcomes. The approach is designed to withstand operational realities (eligibility, compliance, and interagency handoffs) while preserving the core public purpose of subsidy programs: stability, risk reduction, and continuity of housing.
