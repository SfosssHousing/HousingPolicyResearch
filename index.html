Seth Foss

Policy researcher and housing strategist focused on reforming housing subsidy delivery in New York City as public infrastructure - improving integrity, accountability, and household stability through measurable, accountable systems.

Core Policy Idea (in 1 sentence)

Treat housing subsidy delivery as governed public infrastructure with clear compliance standards, measurable performance, and escalation pathways to reduce preventable eviction and downstream public cost.

Policy Summary (what it does)

My approach reframes housing subsidies from "case-by-case" to a systems delivery problem: standardize workflows, tighten documentation and accountability, and build operational dashboards that reveal bottlenecks and prioritize rapid fixes. This approach produces faster, more consistent outcomes for households and lowers long-term public cost.
