Seth Foss - Housing Subsidy Reform Portfolio

Seth Foss

Housing policy researcher and practitioner focused on NYC housing subsidy reform as policy infrastructure: governed, auditable delivery that reduces preventable eviction risk and downstream public cost.

Policy at a glance
  • Problem: Fragmented subsidy workflows and weak accountability create avoidable arrears, instability, and churn.
  • Solution: Standardize documentation + handoffs, enforce time-bound escalation, and measure performance end-to-end.
  • Outcome: Faster, more consistent payments/processing; fewer preventable eviction events; reduced court/shelter pressure.

What I am proposing NYC do

  • Define minimum delivery standards (timelines, documentation, decision logs).
  • Map the full workflow: eligibility -> verification -> approval -> payment -> reconciliation -> disputes.
  • Stand up a simple dashboard: queue age, failure types, resolution time, repeat blockers.
  • Create an escalation protocol for time-sensitive households (clear triggers + ownership).

First 100 days (transition-ready)

  • Run a cross-agency Subsidy Integrity Sprint to identify the top 3 system failures causing arrears/churn.
  • Deploy the escalation protocol + baseline dashboard (even manual first).
  • Publish internal performance targets and assign queue ownership at each handoff.
