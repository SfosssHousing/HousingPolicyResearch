## Ongoing Cadence (Post-Phase IV)

### Weekly
- **Risk radar refresh:** Program Manager reviews emerging risks (operational, legal, fiscal, political); updates risk log and mitigation roadmap.
- **Stakeholder log updates:** Program Manager captures commitments, concerns, and escalations from tenant orgs, agencies, credit unions, legal services. Feed into next review cycle.

### Monthly
- **PET data dashboard:** Data Analyst pulls household enrollment, equity accrual, rent delinquency, building performance metrics from HPD/HRA systems. Posts to internal Notion dashboard for program monitoring.
- **Budget tracking:** Municipal CFO reconciles redirected subsidy outflows (HRA/HPD) vs. equity-investment inflows. Confirm no-new-net-cost constraint is maintained.

### Quarterly
- **Pilot performance review:** Program Manager + HPD/HRA conduct deep-dive on Pilot 1 building. Assess tenant satisfaction, equity outcomes, operational issues. Refine operations playbook based on learning.
- **ROI model refresh:** Data Analyst updates ROI workbook with actual 3-month performance data; rerun scenarios to calibrate long-term projections.
- **Stakeholder check-in:** Program Manager convenes tenant associations, legal-services providers, credit unions, HPD/HRA ops for feedback. Flag any blockers or needed adjustments.

### Annually
- **Comprehensive PET report:** Data Analyst + Civil-Rights Attorney produce annual report on program performance, equity outcomes (disaggregated by race, family size, income), civil-rights compliance, ROI, risk assessment. Public release to NYC Comptroller, Public Advocate, community orgs.
- **Budget re-baseline:** Municipal CFO conducts zero-based review of redirected subsidy streams; assess if additional streams (e.g., NYCHA capital, shelter diversion funds) can be folded into PET.
- **Pilot expansion decision:** Program Manager + leadership team decide whether to launch Pilot 2, Pilot 3 (new buildings or cohorts) based on Pilot 1 outcomes and capacity.
