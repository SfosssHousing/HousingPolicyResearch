# LOCAL LAW D: TCAP IMPLEMENTATION & HPD AUTHORITY

## HPD Authority Amendment and Oversight Framework

______________________________________________________________________

## BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL AS FOLLOWS:

Section 1. The administrative code of the city of New York is amended by adding a new chapter 21 of title 26 to read as follows:

# CHAPTER 21: TCAP PROGRAM IMPLEMENTATION AND HPD AUTHORITY

## § 26-2101 Purpose and HPD Authority

(a) **Purpose**: This chapter establishes explicit statutory authority for HPD to operate the Public Equity Transfer Framework (TCAP) as a permanent, ongoing city program, adopt implementing rules, monitor compliance, enforce affordability and equity outcomes, and coordinate with other city and state agencies.

(b) **Authority**: HPD is authorized to:

(1) Develop and implement TCAP program policy, including member eligibility standards, certification procedures, equity account administration, cooperative oversight, and compliance monitoring.

(2) Adopt rules (within Title 26 of the Administrative Code) defining Qualified Entities, SLEC standards, affordability covenant templates, resale formulas, and enforcement procedures. Rules shall be consistent with Local Laws A–D and subject to public comment and City Council review per § 26-2105.

(3) Establish a TCAP Unit or Division with dedicated staffing, budget authority, and independent decision-making power over TCAP operations.

(4) Allocate annual appropriations to support TCAP member equity funding, technical assistance, training, and cooperative oversight.

(5) Coordinate with DSS on subsidy allocation procedures, income verification, and annual recertification timelines.

(6) Coordinate with NYCHA, HCR, NYC Law Department, DOF, and DOB per interagency MOUs (§ 26-2102).

(7) Provide or fund member training, governance support, and technical assistance to cooperatives at no cost to members.

(8) Monitor and enforce TCAP affordability covenants and program compliance per §§ 26-2106–26-2107.

## § 26-2102 Interagency Memoranda of Understanding

(a) **MOUs Required**: HPD shall negotiate and execute Memoranda of Understanding with the following agencies, each addressing TCAP-specific procedures:

(1) **Department of Social Services (DSS)**: Subsidy allocation mechanics (80/20 split), income verification and recertification procedures, annual subsidy indexing, member support coordination.

(2) **New York City Housing Authority (NYCHA)**: Member recruitment from NYCHA residents, resident association partnerships, governance training coordination, potential maintenance staff training.

(3) **New York State Homes and Community Renewal (HCR)**: Tax credit compliance coordination, state housing program integration, affordability covenant recognition in state-funded projects.

(4) **NYC Department of Law**: Affordability covenant drafting and legal review, enforcement procedure protocols, litigation support for covenant violations.

(5) **NYC Department of Finance (DOF)**: SLEC property tax assessment methodology, abatement and relief eligibility procedures, annual recertification of SLEC status.

(6) **NYC Department of Buildings (DOB)**: Building code compliance monitoring, inspection coordination, violation remediation procedures.

(b) **MOU Procedures**: MOUs shall be executed within 90 days of this law's enactment. Each MOU shall specify:

(1) Roles and responsibilities of each agency.

(2) Information-sharing and coordination procedures.

(3) Dispute resolution mechanisms.

(4) Quarterly or annual review and adjustment processes.

(c) **Public Availability**: All executed MOUs shall be made publicly available on HPD's website within 10 days of execution.

## § 26-2103 Annual TCAP Report to City Council

(a) **Report Requirement**: HPD shall prepare and submit to the City Council an annual TCAP report (due by March 31 each year) containing:

(1) **Buildings and Members**: Number of buildings acquired and converted to TCAP cooperatives (by borough and council district); number of members enrolled and demographic data (race, ethnicity, gender, household income, family composition).

(2) **Subsidy Spending and Member Equity**: Total housing subsidy spending allocated to TCAP (by budget line); average member equity accumulated per member as of December 31 of prior year; range of member equity balances.

(3) **Compliance Status**: Number of active cooperatives; compliance status of each (affordability maintenance, member equity accuracy, governance participation, building maintenance); number of cooperatives subject to compliance notices or enforcement action.

(4) **Enforcement Actions**: Compliance notices issued; compliance orders; receiverships appointed; covenant enforcement actions brought by HPD or member lawsuits; outcomes and remedies.

(5) **Member Outcomes**: Average member income at entry and at Year 5; affordability maintenance (percentage of members paying at 15% cap); voluntary member departures and reasons; member satisfaction data (if available).

(6) **Racial Equity Outcomes**: Racial and ethnic composition of member population vs. program targets (40% Black/Latino); equity accumulation disparities by race (if any); recruiting and retention barriers identified.

(7) **Recommendations**: Program improvements, policy adjustments, budget needs, legislative changes.

(b) **Data Disaggregation**: All quantitative data shall be disaggregated by race, ethnicity, and gender to the extent permitted by privacy law, to enable tracking of equity outcomes and identification of disparities.

(c) **Public Availability**: The report shall be published on HPD's website and made available in hard copy upon request.

(d) **Council Committee Briefings**: HPD shall brief the City Council Housing Committee and Budget Committee quarterly on TCAP progress, financial forecast, any significant issues, and policy recommendations.

## § 26-2104 Rule Making

(a) **Authority**: HPD is authorized to adopt rules implementing this chapter and Local Laws A–D, including:

(1) Member eligibility and income verification procedures.

(2) Qualified Entity designation criteria.

(3) Affordable covenant templates and resale formulas.

(4) Member equity accounting and reporting standards.

(5) Compliance monitoring and enforcement procedures.

(b) **Public Comment**: All rules shall be subject to 30-day public comment periods; HPD shall consider comments and respond to significant concerns before final adoption.

(c) **City Council Review**: Final rules shall be submitted to the City Council for review at least 30 days before implementation; the Council may disapprove rules within that period.

## § 26-2105 Member Equity Compliance Audit

(a) **Annual Audit Requirement**: Every TCAP cooperative shall undergo an annual independent audit of member equity accounts and cooperative financial statements, conducted by a certified public accountant (CPA) licensed to practice in New York State.

(b) **Audit Scope**: Each audit shall verify:

(1) Each member's equity account receives the mandated 20% subsidy redirect monthly (per Local Law A § 26-1803).

(2) Mortgage principal paydown is correctly credited to member accounts.

(3) No member equity was forfeited, seized, or improperly reduced.

(4) Carrying charges assessed to each member did not exceed 15% of that member's verified household income.

(5) All members received quarterly equity statements showing account activity.

(6) TCAP affordability covenant restrictions are maintained and not violated.

(c) **Audit Submission and HPD Review**: Cooperatives shall submit audit reports to HPD within 90 days of year-end. HPD shall review audits and, if discrepancies are identified:

(1) Issue a compliance notice (per § 26-2106) documenting the discrepancy and requiring corrective action.

(2) If discrepancies involve member equity shortfalls, require the cooperative to restore member accounts with interest at the prime rate plus 2%.

(d) **Audit Cost**: Audit costs are a cooperative expense; HPD shall not fund audits but may provide technical assistance in identifying qualified auditors.

## § 26-2106 Compliance Notice and Corrective Action

(a) **Compliance Notice**: If HPD identifies a violation of TCAP covenants or Local Laws A–D, HPD shall issue a written compliance notice:

(1) Describing the specific violation(s).

(2) Explaining the corrective action(s) required.

(3) Providing at least 60 days for the cooperative to achieve compliance or demonstrate a cure plan.

(b) **Cure Opportunity**: The cooperative may cure the violation(s) within the 60-day period by:

(1) Taking corrective action.

(2) Submitting a detailed remediation plan with timeline.

(3) Requesting HPD technical assistance if needed.

(c) **Insufficient Cure**: If the cooperative does not cure or submit a satisfactory plan by the deadline, HPD shall issue a formal Compliance Order.

## § 26-2107 Enforcement and Remedial Action

(a) **Compliance Order**: A formal Compliance Order shall:

(1) Be publicly noticed (copy sent to City Council, member associations, affected members).

(2) Specify the unresolved violation(s).

(3) Require cure within 30 days.

(4) Warn of escalated enforcement if cure is not achieved.

(b) **Remedial Actions Available**:

(1) **Financial Restoration**: Require refund of excess carrying charges to members (with interest at prime + 2%).

(2) **Equity Account Restoration**: Restore member equity accounts to correct balances (with interest).

(3) **Mandatory Governance Action**:
\- Member assembly-supervised board restructuring.
\- Hiring of professional property manager or administrator.
\- Removal of specific board members for cause.
\- Mandatory member training or governance refresher.

(4) **HPD-Supervised Management**: Temporary appointment of HPD-designated manager or administrator to operate the cooperative for 6–12 months pending member restructuring.

(c) **Receivership** (Last Resort): If cure is not achieved and the violation threatens member welfare or building viability (e.g., pervasive financial mismanagement, building maintenance failure, insolvency, or systematic fraud), HPD may petition the Supreme Court to appoint a receiver for the cooperative. The receiver shall:

(1) Operate the cooperative pending member vote to restructure governance.

(2) If member restructuring is feasible, facilitate member control restoration.

(3) If cooperative is insolvent or irreparable, liquidate assets and distribute member equity balances equitably.

(d) **Affordability Covenant Enforcement**: If a cooperative attempts to remove affordability protections, convert units to market rate, or charge above-affordability carrying charges, HPD and the NYC Law Department shall jointly bring action for injunctive relief and specific performance to enforce the covenant. Members may also bring such action independently.

(e) **Timeline**: HPD shall complete initial investigation and issue compliance notice within 30 days of learning of potential violation. HPD shall seek court-ordered receiver appointment, if necessary, within 120 days of issuing a Compliance Order if cure is not achieved.

## § 26-2108 Fair Housing and Non-Discrimination Enforcement

(a) **HPD Monitoring**: HPD shall monitor TCAP cooperatives for compliance with fair housing laws (42 U.S.C. § 3604), New York State Human Rights Law, and NYC Human Rights Law, including:

(1) Member admissions practices (looking for discriminatory screening).

(2) Board composition and election procedures (looking for exclusion of protected groups).

(3) Member complaint procedures and resolutions.

(4) Lease enforcement and eviction patterns (looking for disparate impact).

(b) **Complaint Investigation**: When HPD receives a discrimination complaint from a member or third party, HPD shall:

(1) Investigate and provide findings within 60 days.

(2) If discrimination is substantiated, issue a compliance notice and require corrective action (e.g., retraction of discriminatory decision, compensatory damages, policy change).

(3) Refer substantiated cases to the NYC Commission on Human Rights for concurrent investigation and enforcement.

(c) **Public Reporting**: HPD shall report discrimination complaints and substantiated findings in its annual TCAP report (§ 26-2103), disaggregated by protected class.

## § 26-2109 TCAP Program Sunset and Review

(a) **Mandatory Review**: Within 10 years of this law's enactment, HPD shall commission an independent evaluation of the TCAP program, including:

(1) Member outcomes (wealth accumulation, affordability, governance participation, satisfaction).

(2) Cooperative sustainability and financial health.

(3) Equity outcomes (racial composition, wealth-building disparities, affordability maintenance).

(4) Fiscal impact (subsidy spend vs. member wealth created; comparison to alternatives).

(5) Lessons learned and recommendations for expansion or modification.

(b) **Program Continuation**: Absent affirmative repeal by the City Council, the TCAP program shall continue indefinitely beyond the 10-year review. The evaluation shall be used to inform potential legislative amendments or improvements.

## § 26-2110 Implementation and Effective Date

This chapter shall become effective upon enactment; HPD shall adopt implementing rules and execute interagency MOUs within 90 days.
